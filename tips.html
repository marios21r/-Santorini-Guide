Tips for Visiting Santorini

Book Everything in Advance

Especially for sunset times, ensure to book your tours, restaurants, and accommodations well in advance to avoid disappointment.

Visit a Winery

Santorini is famous for its wine, particularly the Assyrtiko variety. Visit a local winery for a tour and tasting.

Expect Delays

During high season months, there can be significant delays at the airport and port. Plan your travel times accordingly.

Cash Only

Some businesses and stores, including buses, only accept cash. Always carry some cash with you.

Use Uber Instead of Taxis

Using Uber can help you control your spending more effectively compared to traditional taxis.

Best Time to Visit

Visit Santorini in late May, early June, or early September for pleasant weather and fewer crowds.

Explore a Traditional Village

Ensure you explore at least one traditional village to experience the authentic culture and charm of Santorini.

Stay Hydrated

The weather can be quite hot, so carry water with you and stay hydrated, especially during your outdoor activities.

No Pavement in Most Roads

Be careful while walking as many roads lack proper pavement.

Oia Sunset

The Oia sunset is considered the best. Ensure to find a good spot early as it gets crowded.

Santorini's Small Tomatoes

Try the local small tomatoes; they have a unique and delicious taste.

Red Beach

Red Beach is a must-visit for its unique landscape and stunning views.

Many Stairs in Villages

Most traditional villages and all villages on the caldera side have many stairs up and down. Be prepared for a lot of walking.

Take a Boat Tour

Take a boat tour to explore the caldera and nearby islands. It's a great way to see more of Santorini's beauty.

Visit Akrotiri

Visit the Archaeological Site of Akrotiri to learn about the ancient Minoan civilization and see well-preserved ruins.
