Santorini Hidden Villages

Discover the charm and beauty of Santorini's hidden traditional villages. These gems are often overlooked by tourists but offer a glimpse into the island's rich history and culture. Many of these villages are accessible only by narrow paths, preserving their unique charm and traditional architecture. Explore these hidden treasures and immerse yourself in the authentic Santorini experience.

Megalochori Village

Megalochori is a picturesque village with narrow, winding paths and traditional Cycladic houses. Not accessible by car, this village offers a peaceful and authentic atmosphere with charming tavernas and local wineries.

Kontochori Village

Kontochori is known for its beautiful gardens and traditional houses. Not accessible by car, this village is a hidden gem with a rich cultural heritage and stunning views of the island.

Pyrgos Village

Pyrgos is a medieval village with a labyrinth of narrow paths and charming houses. Not accessible by car, it offers panoramic views of the island from its hilltop location and is home to several historic churches.

Emporio Village

Emporio is one of the largest villages on Santorini, known for its medieval Kasteli (castle) and winding streets. It offers a blend of history and tradition with vibrant local life.

Foinikia Village

Foinikia is a quaint village near Oia, famous for its traditional cave houses and serene atmosphere. Not accessible by car, it provides a tranquil escape with stunning sunset views.
